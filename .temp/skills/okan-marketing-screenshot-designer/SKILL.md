# Marketing Screenshot Designer

Use Figma as the source of truth for App Store and Google Play screenshot sets.

## Operating Model

- Start by inspecting current product screens, marketing Figma files, brand direction, store copy, and approved screenshot sets before designing.
- Create Figma screenshot frames only; do not implement code or prepare store-console submission fields.
- Preserve approved screenshot sets unless the user explicitly asks to replace or update them.
- Use product-realistic screens and app mockups; do not invent impossible app states or unsupported features.
- Keep screenshot headlines short, concrete, benefit-led, and readable at store-preview scale.

## Scope

Use this skill for:

- App Store and Google Play screenshot sets.
- Screenshot sequence planning and benefit-led screenshot headlines.
- App mockup layouts, device frames, and product proof compositions.
- Feature-focused screenshot variants and export-ready Figma frames.
- Screenshot QA for readability, platform consistency, and product realism.

Do not use this skill for:

- App Store or Google Play listing text metadata, keywords, descriptions, or release notes.
- App Store Connect submission, privacy labels, compliance, TestFlight, or review responses.
- Landing pages, social graphics, ads, launch graphics, or broad campaign assets.
- In-app product screen design, onboarding flows, design-system foundations, component-library cleanup, frontend code, backend, or mobile implementation.

## Screenshot Design Rules

- Sequence screenshots from broad value to concrete feature proof.
- Prefer one clear message per screenshot.
- Use real or faithful product screens and realistic data examples.
- Keep visual hierarchy strong when zoomed out.
- Use platform-specific frame names and keep App Store and Google Play sets separate.
- Avoid unsupported claims about AI, automation, availability, pricing, privacy, or guaranteed outcomes.

## Figma Workflow

- Follow the required Figma execution skill before any Figma write action.
- Inspect existing marketing/store pages before creating or replacing frames.
- Place new variants near the source set and keep approved sets unchanged unless asked.
- Use export-friendly frame names such as `App Store / Screenshot 01` or `Google Play / Screenshot 03`.
- Use Auto Layout deliberately: fixed sizes for export frames and device mockups, `Hug contents` for headline/copy groups, and `Fill container` for full-width layout regions.

## QA Rules

- Before reporting completion, check zoomed-out headline readability, clipped text, collapsed frames, missing fonts, safe-area issues, export frame naming, and platform-specific consistency.
- Confirm app screens show product-realistic content and do not conflict with known app behavior.
- Report created or updated frames, sequence rationale, any copy assumptions, and any claims that need confirmation.
