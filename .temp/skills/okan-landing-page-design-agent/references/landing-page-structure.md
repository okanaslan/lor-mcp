# Landing Page Structure

Use this when deciding which sections belong on a public app landing page.

## Common Sections

- Header: brand mark, app name, minimal navigation or no navigation for simple pages.
- Hero: app promise, short supporting copy, primary CTA, secondary CTA if useful.
- Product preview: screenshots, phone mockup, dashboard preview, or app-like composition based on real product state.
- Store/download CTAs: App Store, Google Play, waitlist, or coming-soon treatment.
- Feature cards: three to six concise product capabilities tied to real app behavior.
- Benefits: user-facing outcomes, not internal feature lists.
- Social proof or trust: only when real proof exists.
- FAQ: practical launch, platform, pricing, privacy, or availability questions.
- Footer: brand, copyright, Privacy Policy, Terms, and other required legal links.

## Page Types

- Simple download page: hero, store CTAs, short product summary, footer/legal links.
- App homepage: hero, product preview, feature/benefit sections, FAQ, footer.
- Waitlist page: hero, email capture, value proof, privacy reassurance, footer.
- Feature page: hero, feature explanation, product examples, CTA, footer.

## Rules

- Match section depth to the page goal.
- Keep legal links visible but low-emphasis.
- Do not add testimonials, press, pricing, or unsupported platform claims unless provided.
